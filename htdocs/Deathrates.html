Global Death Rates

Air pollution contributes to 11.65% of global deaths or approximately 6.67 million deaths. At least one of the risk factors attributed to these deaths is air pollution, meaning other risk factors, such as smoking, could have contributed to death. Air pollution ranks third on the most number of deaths caused by a risk factor, with high blood pressure ranking first, and smoking ranking second.





Countrywide Death Rates

In the Philippines, air pollution ranks fourth in the most number of deaths caused by a risk factor. The Philippines' share of death to air pollution has changed over the past 30 years. In the latest data of 2019, 12.1% of the Philippines' total number of deaths are attributed to air pollution. Starting from 1990, the highest death rate percentage of air pollution recorded in the Philippines was during the year 2010, with a percentage of 15.38%. The lowest percentage recorded was during 2019, the first year the pandemic hit.
